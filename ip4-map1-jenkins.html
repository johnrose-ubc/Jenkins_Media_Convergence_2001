Media Convergence

“An ongoing process, occuring at various intersections of media technologies, industries, content and audiences; it's not an end state.”
— Henry Jenkins, 2001
⊕ Technological Convergence
  • Negroponte’s “atoms to bits”
  • Digitization of all media content
  • Expands relationships between media and enables flow across platforms
⊕ Economic Convergence
  • Horizontal integration of the entertainment industry
  • AOL Time Warner controls film, television, books, games, web, music, real estate
  • Cultural production restructured around “synergies”
  • Allows exploitation of branded properties across media platforms
⊕ Organic Convergence
  • The new media environment requires multitasking strategies
  • We get an unintentional mash-up of our simultaneous inputs
  • The convergence is internal because we are the focal point for all the inputs
⊕ Cultural Convergence
  • Technologies, industries, and sub-cultures intersect → new forms of creativity emerge
  • “Archive, annotate, appropriate, recirculate” → a new participatory folk culture
  • Co-opted as cheap user-generated content by marketers seeking brand loyalty
  • Transmedia storytelling exploits each medium’s strengths
  • Virtuous cycle with organic convergence as multitasking is normalized
⊕ Global Convergence
  • Digital media is frictionless and relatively borderless
  • Multinational economic convergence accelerates this
  • Previously local genres discovered internationally (world music, Asian cinema)
  • These enter the organic convergence mash-up → rise of the untravelled global citizen
